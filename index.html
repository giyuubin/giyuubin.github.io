Abstract

This page documents ongoing coursework and undergraduate research in applied cryptography, centered on the algebraic hardness assumptions underlying post-quantum key-encapsulation and signature schemes — ML-KEM and ML-DSA — and their transition path from classical public-key primitives. Work is carried out as an undergraduate researcher at the Information Security and Privacy Lab (ISPL), Gachon University.

Keywords — lattice-based cryptography, learning with errors, ML-KEM / ML-DSA, hash-based signatures, applied cryptography

Def. 1 — Search-LWE
